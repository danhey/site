I'm Dan Hey, a PhD student at the University of Sydney working on asteroseismology – the study of stellar pulsations. Using the Kepler, TESS, and Gaia space satellites, I investigate the abundance and population statistics of pulsating A/F type stars, including the δ Scuti and roAp stars. I am particularly interested in using pulsations as a clock to observe orbital companions, as well as using eclipsing binaries to obtain precise fundamental parameters. Besides astronomy, I am passionate about open-source code, mango icecream, bouldering, and figure skating.

Projects

    Maelstrom [code] maelstrom is a set of custom PyMC3 Models and solvers for modelling binary orbits through the phase modulation technique. Echelle [code] echelle is a fun Python package for plotting and interacting with echelle diagrams, which are stacked power spectra of a star's light curve. CMDTess [code] Have you ever wanted to look at the amplitude spectrum of every southern hemisphere TESS 2-minute target? Alpha Draconis [blog] It turns out alpha Draconis is a detached eclipsing binary, and one of the brightest that we've found! Check out our NASA press release.
